# ЁЯзм My Journey

My journey started in **Electrical Engineering**, where digital logic and circuit design trained me to think algorithmically long before I wrote production software. Working with complex systems taught me how to reason through layered architectures, trace failures precisely, and stay comfortable inside complexity.

After several years working as a frontend, backend, and eventually full-stack developer, I realized that writing clean code alone is not enough to build systems people genuinely trust. To understand how successful technical products are designed and managed at scale, I completed my first Master's degree in **IT Management**.

Later, while building software for clinics and healthcare environments, I saw clearly that the future of medical systems would be deeply connected with Artificial Intelligence. But integrating existing AI models into software was only part of the challenge. To truly improve these systems, contribute meaningful research, and help build AI tools capable of supporting clinical decision-making responsibly, I needed a deeper understanding of the mathematics and learning theory behind modern AI.

That realization brought me to Germany and to my second Master's degree in **Intelligent Adaptive Systems at Universit├дt Hamburg**.

Today, my work combines:
- research thinking,
- production engineering,
- healthcare domain experience,
- and applied machine learning.

A PhD is the natural next step in that journey.
